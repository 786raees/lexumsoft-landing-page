# Upwork Proposal Best Practices

## What Hiring Managers Look For

1. **Specificity**: Reference details from their job post. Generic proposals get ignored.
2. **Relevant experience**: Show you've done exactly this type of work before, with numbers.
3. **Clear deliverables**: List exactly what they'll get and when.
4. **Professional tone**: Confident but not arrogant. Friendly but not casual.
5. **Reasonable pricing**: Justified by expertise, not the cheapest bid.

## Upwork Proposal Limits

- **Cover letter**: 5,000 characters max (but shorter is better -- aim for 200-400 words)
- **Attachments**: Up to 10 files (use for portfolio pieces, case study PDFs)
- **Questions**: Answer all screening questions before the cover letter

## Keyword Optimization

Upwork's relevance algorithm considers proposal text. Include these naturally:
- The exact job title keywords
- Service-specific terms (SEO, Google Ads, website design, etc.)
- Industry terms (plumber, HVAC, contractor, home service)

## Proposal Structure for Maximum Impact

### First 2 Lines (Most Critical)
These appear in the preview before the client clicks "View Proposal." They must:
- Acknowledge their specific problem (shows you read the job post)
- Include a relevant credential or stat

### Middle Section
- One case study with specific numbers
- 3-4 bullet approach (what you'll do)
- Deliverables + timeline

### Closing
- Price with brief justification
- Risk reversal (guarantee if applicable)
- Soft CTA ("Happy to discuss" -- not pushy)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Copy-paste proposals**: Clients can tell. Always reference specifics from their job post.
2. **Leading with price**: Lead with value, then mention price.
3. **Too long**: Most clients scan, not read. Keep it focused.
4. **No proof**: Always include at least one case study with numbers.
5. **Desperate tone**: Never say "I really need this job" or undercut pricing drastically.
6. **Ignoring screening questions**: Always answer them first -- some clients auto-reject if skipped.

## Follow-Up Best Practices

- Wait 3 days before following up
- Add value in the follow-up (don't just ask "did you see my proposal?")
- Offer a quick insight about their current website/GBP/ads as a conversation starter
- One follow-up only -- don't pester
