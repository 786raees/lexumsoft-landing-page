## Writing Rules

### Post Body Arc
1. **Hook** (lines 1-3): The MOST IMPORTANT part. Must psychologically compel scrolling. Use contrast hooks, specific numbers, loss aversion, pattern interrupts, or confession hooks. No questions -- statements hit harder. Each line under 10 words. First line must create an emotional reaction in under 2 seconds.
2. **Problem** (with stats): Industry-wide data that makes the reader think "that's probably me"
3. **Math** (show the calculator): Plug-in-your-own-numbers format. "30 leads/month x $600 avg ticket..."
4. **Empathy** ("I know why"): Show you understand they're under a sink, not ignoring leads
5. **Anecdote** (client story): Name, state, company size, specific before/after numbers
6. **Solution** (LexumSoft system): 2-3 sentences on what was built, not a sales pitch
7. **CTA** ("Comment KEYWORD"): What they get (audit, breakdown, etc.)
8. **Close** ("No pitch. Just the numbers."): Low pressure

### Character Count Discipline
- **Hard limit:** 3,000 characters
- **Target:** 2,800-2,950 characters
- **Count includes:** spaces, line breaks, `---` separators, hashtags
- **If over 3,000:** Cut from problem elaboration (middle section), never from hook, anecdote, or CTA
- **Each `---` separator:** ~4 characters
- **10 hashtags:** ~200-250 characters typically

### No Em-Dashes
- NEVER use em-dashes (the long dash character). Always use double hyphens (--) instead.
- This applies to the post body, first comment, deployment notes, and DM templates.

### Hashtag Rules
- Always 8-12 hashtags on a single line at the end
- **Always include:** #PlumbingBusiness #HVACBusiness #ContractorMarketing #PlumbingMarketing #HVACMarketing
- Add 3-7 topic-specific tags (e.g., #SpeedToLead, #LeadConversion, #GoogleBusinessProfile)
